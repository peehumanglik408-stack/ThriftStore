👥 Demographics Analysis

Age, gender, city tier, and income distributions — who is your customer base in India?

📌 18–27 is the dominant cohort (~55%), confirming Gen Z and early millennials as your primary audience. Strong Metro and Tier 2 presence validates an Instagram-first digital strategy over offline-first.
Purchase Intent by Age Group
📌 The 18–22 and 23–27 cohorts show the highest "Interested" proportions — these are your core conversion audience. The 23–27 cohort has a large "Maybe" segment — convertible with the right social proof or one-time discount. Older segments (35+) skew toward lower intent.

👗 Fashion Behaviour Analysis

Style identity, shopping frequency, sustainability attitudes, and channel preferences — the primary clustering variables defining your segments.

📌 Y2K/Retro and Streetwear together account for ~28% each — making them the top priority for your first inventory builds. Minimal/Clean girl represents higher-income buyers with larger spend capacity. Cottagecore/Boho is an emerging trend with low competition in Indian thrift.
📌 "Fashion as self-expression" is the dominant identity (~30%), directly validating the brand's positioning around unique, curated aesthetics. The "Avoid fast fashion actively" cohort (~18%) aligns with your sustainability narrative.
Sustainability & Shopping Frequency
📌 Instagram/Reels dominates at ~42% — perfectly validating your Instagram-first strategy. WhatsApp drops at ~20% suggest a strong community group / broadcast channel strategy should run in parallel.
Style × Purchase Intent Cross-Analysis
📌 Y2K/Retro and Streetwear show the highest "Interested" proportions, confirming these are your highest-converting style segments. Formal/Classic skews toward "Not Interested" — lower ROI for formal workwear drops early on.

🛍️ Product Preferences Analysis

Clothing types, colour palettes, accessories, and occasion drivers — feeds association rule mining for bundle strategy.

Clothing Type Preferences
📌 Oversized tees and baggy jeans lead by a wide margin (~55–60%), confirming streetwear and casual aesthetics as your highest-volume inventory categories. Jackets/blazers and crop tops are "cross-aesthetic" items that serve multiple customer types and reduce inventory risk.
Colour Palette Preferences
Accessory Interest
📌 Jewellery and bags are the top accessory interests — both easy to source thrifted and high-margin. Bundle accessories into outfit drops to increase average order value.
Occasion Drivers & Bundle Preference
📌 Everyday/casual wear leads at ~72% — confirming daily-wear items should dominate inventory. Festivals at ~52% is crucial for India — plan themed drops 4–6 weeks before Diwali, Holi, and Navratri.

♻️ Thrift Attitudes & Barriers

Prior experience, purchase barriers, discovery channels, and motivators — your conversion funnel.

📌 A significant share has prior thrift experience — both "regularly" and "a few times" segments are your warmest leads. The "open but haven't yet" cohort is the highest-priority acquisition target.
📌 Strong upcycling interest validates your upcycled originals product line as a brand differentiator with premium pricing potential.
📌 Return policy sensitivity is a significant purchase barrier. Prioritise COD and easy 7-day returns as a trust-builder in your early months — the incremental logistics cost is worth the conversion uplift.
